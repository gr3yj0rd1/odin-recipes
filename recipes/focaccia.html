Focaccia

Rosemary focaccia is an easy and tasty recipe, ideal as an appetizer or for a day trip.

Ingredients

  • 400g flour
  • 7g fresh yeast
  • 140g potatoes
  • 180ml fresh water (30 ml for the brine)
  • 10g sugar
  • 6g salt
  • pepper
  • rosemary
  • 70g olive oil (50 for the dough + 20 for the brine)

Steps

  1. We put all the dough ingredients except the salt in a large bowl. Then we pour in: the flour, water, oil, sugar, mashed boiled potato and the sachet of PANEANGELI Mastro Fornaio Brewer's Yeast. We work all the ingredients with a spoon.
  2. Once you have formed an irregular dough, transfer it to a pastry board and add the salt, then work it by hand for another 5 minutes.
  3. Once you have obtained a smooth and homogeneous dough, transfer it to a bowl, make the classic cross cut, cover with cling film and leave to rise for 2 hours or until doubled in volume.
  4. We spread the dough with our hands directly in a baking pan (mine measures 30 cm x 40 cm) lightly greased with oil. Let the focaccia rise in the pan for another half hour.
  5. In the meantime, prepare the brine that will season the focaccia. Mix the water, oil, chopped rosemary and pepper in a small bowl.
  6. After the second rising time, press the focaccia with your fingertips to form many small dimples and sprinkle it with the brine using a brush. Sprinkle the focaccia with a pinch of coarse salt and bake in a static oven preheated to 200 Â°C for 15 minutes.
  7. Here is the rosemary focaccia served on the table! I recommend you eat it very hot, but even cold the next day it is really delicious!
